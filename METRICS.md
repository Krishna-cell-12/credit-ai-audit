# Metrics Framework — AI Spend Auditor

> **Version:** 1.0 | **Status:** Draft  
> **Philosophy:** We track what we intend to move. Every metric must have an owner, a target, and a decision rule — if it drops below threshold X, we do Y. No vanity metrics.

---

## The Three North Star Metrics

These are the three metrics that, if all trending positively simultaneously, signal that the business is healthy at its core. All other metrics cascade from these.

---

### ⭐ North Star #1 — Lead Gate Conversion Rate (LGCR)

**Definition:** The percentage of unique visitors who enter their email to receive their AI spend audit report.

```
LGCR = (Emails Captured) / (Unique Landing Page Visitors) × 100
```

**Why it matters:**  
This is the single most important measure of product-market fit at the top of the funnel. It tells us whether our value proposition is compelling enough to overcome friction. A low LGCR means the market doesn't believe the premise yet — no amount of paid traffic or influencer reach fixes a broken value prop.

| Signal | LGCR Value | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 🔴 Critical | < 15% | Rewrite headline copy; A/B test value prop framing |
| 🟡 Needs work | 15–30% | Optimize above-the-fold; improve social proof |
| 🟢 Healthy | 30–50% | Scale traffic acquisition; begin paid experiments |
| 🚀 Exceptional | > 50% | Double down on all acquisition channels; protect conversion |

**Measurement:** PostHog / Mixpanel funnel event: `landing_page_view` → `email_submitted`  
**Owner:** Head of Product  
**Review cadence:** Weekly (daily during launch week)  
**Target at launch:** ≥ 35%

---

### ⭐ North Star #2 — Viral K-Factor (Viral Coefficient)

**Definition:** The number of new users generated by each existing user through organic sharing.

```
K-Factor = (Avg. Shares per User) × (Conversion Rate of Shared Link)

If K > 1.0 → Viral growth (compounding loop)
If K = 0.5 → Each 100 users generates 50 more → additive, not exponential
If K < 0.3 → Loop is effectively broken; growth is linear only
```

**Why it matters:**  
The viral loop is the primary CAC reduction mechanism in our model. K-factor is the heartbeat of the growth engine. Every percentage point improvement in K-factor has a multiplicative effect on total lead volume over time. This metric also validates our product design: if users are sharing, the report is generating genuine "wow" moments.

**Component metrics to track:**
| Sub-Metric | Formula | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Share Rate | Users who share / Total users who receive report | ≥ 25% |
| Shared Link CTR | Clicks on shared link / Times link is shared | ≥ 30% |
| Shared Link → Email Conv. | Emails from shared link / Clicks on shared link | ≥ 45% |
| **K-Factor** | **Share Rate × Shared Link CTR × Shared Link → Email Conv.** | **≥ 0.5 (target: 0.7)** |

**Measurement:** Custom UTM tracking + referral source tagging on every email capture  
**Owner:** Growth Lead / Founder  
**Review cadence:** Weekly  
**Decision rule:** If K-Factor < 0.3 for 2 consecutive weeks → sprint on share UX (copy, incentive, report design)

### ⭐ North Star #3 — Tool Redundancy Hit Rate (TRHR)

**Definition:** The percentage of audited users for whom the tool identifies at least one actionable redundancy (two or more tools with > 60% functional overlap in the user's stack).

```
TRHR = (Audits with ≥ 1 Detected Redundancy) / (Total Audits Completed) × 100
```

**Why it matters:**  
This is the core **product utility metric**. If TRHR is low, one of two things is true: (a) our target market doesn't actually have overlapping tools, which would invalidate the product thesis, or (b) our detection logic is weak. High TRHR means the problem is real, the product is working, and users will have a reason to share ("I can't believe I was wasting $X — look at this!"). TRHR directly drives virality.

| Signal | TRHR Value | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| 🔴 Critical | < 40% | Widen detection model; revisit ICP; check data quality |
| 🟡 Needs work | 40–60% | Refine overlap taxonomy; add more tool categories |
| 🟢 Healthy | 60–75% | Core thesis validated; optimize detection precision |
| 🚀 Exceptional | > 75% | Product is finding real pain; aggressively expand tool coverage |

**Measurement:** Server-side event on audit completion: `redundancy_detected: true/false`  
**Owner:** Head of Product  
**Review cadence:** Weekly  
**Target at launch:** ≥ 65%

## Supporting Metrics Dashboard

These are the metrics that explain *why* the North Stars are moving. Monitor weekly; investigate on variance > 20% week-over-week.

### Acquisition Layer
| Metric | Formula | Target | Alert Threshold |
|---|---|---|---|
| Organic Traffic | Unique visitors from non-paid sources | +15%/week (launch) | < 0%/week |
| Paid Traffic CAC | Ad spend / Leads from paid | < $15/lead | > $25/lead |
| Top Referral Source | Which source sends the most qualified traffic | — | Monitor for shifts |
| Time to First Report | From email submit → report generated | < 60 seconds | > 3 minutes |

### Activation Layer
| Metric | Formula | Target | Alert Threshold |
|---|---|---|---|
| Report Completion Rate | Users who view full report / Users who submit email | ≥ 75% | < 50% |
| Avg. Redundancies Detected | Sum of redundancies / Total audits | ≥ 1.8 per user | < 0.9 per user |
| Avg. Estimated Savings Surfaced | Avg. $/month shown in report | ≥ $120/month | < $50/month |
| Recommendation Click-Through | Clicks on affiliate recommendation links / Reports viewed | ≥ 20% | < 8% |

### Retention & Revenue Layer
| Metric | Formula | Target | Alert Threshold |
|---|---|---|---|
| Free → Pro Conversion Rate | Pro signups / Free users (30-day window) | ≥ 8% | < 3% |
| Monthly Churn Rate | Cancelled subscribers / Active subscribers | ≤ 4% | > 8% |
| Affiliate Revenue per Active User | Affiliate commissions / MAU | ≥ $2/user | < $0.50/user |
| Net Revenue Retention (NRR) | MRR from retained + expansion / Prior period MRR | ≥ 105% | < 95% |

### Viral Health Layer
| Metric | Formula | Target | Alert Threshold |
|---|---|---|---|
| Share Rate | Users sharing / Users receiving report | ≥ 25% | < 10% |
| Referral Attribution Rate | Leads with known referral source / Total leads | ≥ 85% | < 70% |
| Viral Loop Cycle Time | Avg. time from share to new email capture | < 48 hours | > 7 days |
